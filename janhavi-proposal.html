01
Instagram is undermonetised
987K followers with no structured CTA funnel, no lead magnet in bio, and no system pushing followers into a course purchase journey. The audience is warm. It's just not being directed anywhere.
02

733 videos and 2.43M subs — but no modern hook structure, no consistent thumbnail system, and no Shorts strategy compounding reach. The channel built its subscriber base years ago. It hasn't been optimised since.
03
Ads are running — but not efficiently
The webinar-to-course funnel is a smart structure. The problem is in the execution — too many campaigns, creatives that are too long, and spend that likely isn't being tracked against actual course revenue. More ads isn't the answer. Better ads is.
04
No consistent brand identity
At 987K Instagram and 2.43M YouTube, there's no cohesive visual language tying both platforms together — no consistent colour palette, typography, thumbnail style, or content format that screams Janhavi Panwar before someone even reads the name. A brand at this scale deserves a brand system to match.
05
No signature IP on either platform
IP — Intellectual Property — in content terms means a specific format or series that only you make, that people recognise instantly, and that creates brand recall every time it appears. Think MrBeast's challenge format, or Tanmay Bhat's roast structure. Right now both your Instagram and YouTube are collections of individual videos — there's no recurring series, no signature format, nothing that makes a new viewer say "oh, this is the Janhavi Panwar thing." That's the gap between a creator and a brand. At your audience size, this is the highest-leverage thing you're missing.
